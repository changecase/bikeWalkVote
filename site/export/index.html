We Did It!

Thanks to the support of people like you, Bike Walk Vote PAC had our most successful election night in our brief history.

We made nine endorsements this primary season. Seven of those candidates won their races, and the other two made it to November runoff elections. We went undefeated!

Our Victories

  • Jeff Reardon

    Electing Jeff Reardon as the Democratic nominee in House District 48. Reardon took out a ten-year incumbent, the vehemently pro-CRC Mike Schaufler. It was the first time in nearly 20 years that a Democratic incumbent has been taken out in the primary.

  • Bob Stacey

    Electing Bob Stacey to the Metro Council. Stacey is a lifelong advocate for livable communities and transportation choices, and an eloquent opponent of the CRC highway mega-project.

  • Sam Chase

    Electing Sam Chase to the Metro Council. Affordability advocate Chase got over 58% of the vote in a five-person race, avoiding a November runoff.

  • Dick Schouten

    Re-electing Commissioner Dick Schouten in Washington County. Schouten, the leading voice for transportation choices in Oregon’s second-most populous county, beat back a well-funded opponent to earn re-election.

Runoff

The runoff results are no less impressive.

  • Jefferson Smith

    We helped Jefferson Smith overcome low starting poll numbers and being significantly outspent to make it to the November runoff. Jefferson used anti-CRC messaging in television ads to demonstrate his leadership and shared priorities with Portlanders.

  • Mary Nolan

    We worked with Mary Nolan to go from being a virtual unknown to fight the incumbent City Councilor to a dead heat. This is the first time in 26 years a sitting Portland Councilor has been taken to a runoff.

We have a good chance to win these races in November if we continue to devote significant time, money, and energy to the campaigns.

Thank you for helping making this happen. We couldn’t have done it without you.
