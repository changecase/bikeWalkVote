  • Bike Walk Vote Endorses Jefferson Smith for Mayor

    We’ve officially endorsed Jefferson Smith for Portland Mayor.

    Smith sets himself apart from the other candidates with his compelling commitment to promoting equitable transportation investments in underserved Portland communities and his unequivocal fiscal responsibility around the proposed Columbia River Crossing, where he supports the smartest path for saving the most taxpayer dollars while moving people and freight.

  • PSU’s Lincoln Hall the battleground for mayoral debate

    Desmond Fuller of PSU’s Vanguard also covered Local Motion.

    Portland is one of the most pedestrian/bike and public transit friendly cities in the country, a fact supported over the last few years by the city consistently ranking high in various top-10 lists (such as Biking Magazine and reports from the Department of Transportation). This makes transportation one of the most important issues in the race to replace outgoing Mayor Sam Adams.

    Read the full article.

  • Portland mayoral candidates compete to praise bicycling, walking and transit

    Jeff Mapes of the Oregonian covered PSU Planning Club’s mayoral forum event Local Motion: Portland Mayoral Candidates Discuss Active Transportation.

    We were there too. We organized a pre-party at Hotlips Pizza, an after-party at Rogue, and we lead the twitter coverage (#PDXMayor).

    On Monday night I was at one of the countless number of forums featuring the three major candidates for Portland mayor. This event – sponsored by nine groups supportive of cycling, walking and transit – packed the main auditorium in Portland State University’s 475-seat Lincoln Hall and was a good example of how interest groups can use these kinds of forums to prod candidates in their direction.
